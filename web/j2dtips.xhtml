How to get olive oil out of a shirt?

Blot any excess oil using a clean cloth or a paper towel. Then, squirt some aloe vera, dish soap, or
shampoo onto the stain. Use an old toothbrush or a manicure brush to rub the stain into the fabric.
Let the stain sit for a few minutes.

How do you get truck grease out of clothes?

how to remove rust stain

If you've got rust on clothing it can be removed with either white vinegar or lemon juice.
Lay the clothing or fabric out on an old towel and pour a small amount of white vinegar directly
on the stain - or rub a cut lemon half on the stain.
Saturate it thoroughly, then blot it with a clean white towel.

method 2

Moisten a soft cloth or cotton ball with white vinegar.
Blot the stained area to moisten it completely.
Cover the stain with a thin layer of salt.
Rub the vinegar and salt together onto the stain with your fingers.
If possible, lay the piece on top of a towel in direct sunlight.

How to get gum off clothes

Cover the area affected by gum in liquid laundry soap.
Use a toothbrush to work the laundry soap into the gum. This will break it up.
Use a blunt knife and gently scrape away at the gum.
Lastly, use your fingernail to scrape the remaining gum off the fabric.
Throw the garment in the washing machine.

how to remove ink

make a baking soda paste (two parts baking soda to 1 part water) is also a good trick to help lift blood stains.
Apply the poultice directly to the stain and leave it for up to 30 minutes. After you've removed the paste,
carefully blot the area with a damp cloth or paper towel then wash as normal.

how to remove blood stains

Again, start by blotting the stain to remove excess ink. You’ll need a microfibre cloth rather than paper towels,
because bits of paper can get caught in the garment.Apply an aerosol hairspray or some rubbing alcohol directly onto the stain.
The products contain alcohol, which can be really effective in breaking down the oil in this type of stain.Antibacterial hand
wash can also do the same job. Just make sure you wear protective clothing and use them in a well-ventilated room.
While the rubbing alcohol, hand wash, or hair spray are still wet, put the garment straight into the washing machine
and wash it as normal. Try to wash it by itself if you can.Make sure the stain is completely gone before you air dry it.

How do you remove deodorant stains from clothes?

Just pour a bit of vinegar directly onto the stain, and rub it into the fabric before placing
the item in the wash. You can also remove deodorant stains from your washable shirts and blouses
